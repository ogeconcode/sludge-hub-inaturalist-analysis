Overview and Context

This work is supported by the **The Sludge Hub & Company**. As described on their website, "The Sludge Hub & Company is a network of Abandoned Mine Lands (AML) that hosts ecological restoration and waste reutilization projects. It provides access, support services, and community to a wide range of eco-concerned organizations and individuals operating in post-coal spaces." You can learn more at their official website: www.sludgehub.org.

This webpage was created specifically to monitor environmental changes within the Mineral County, WV region. A key component of these ecological monitoring efforts involves documenting the flora and fauna present, with a particular focus on **pollinator conservation**. iNaturalist serves as an invaluable tool for citizen science data collection, helping us understand the distribution and diversity of species crucial for healthy ecosystems in post-coal landscapes.

These maps help us visualize several key aspects:

  • **Property Boundaries:** Observations directly within our defined research property.
  • **Buffer Zones:** Observations within specified distances (1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 miles) from our primary research center and a key apiary location. These buffers help us understand the broader ecological context and potential influences beyond our immediate boundaries.
  • **Species Distribution:** Where various species have been observed, providing insights into their spatial patterns.

Methodology and Judgment Calls

To ensure transparency in our data processing and map generation, we want to clarify some of the key decisions and steps taken:

  • **Data Filtering:** iNaturalist observations were filtered to include only "Research Grade" observations to ensure higher data quality. Observations lacking precise coordinates or identified to a very broad taxonomic level may have been excluded.
  • **Coordinate Precision:** While iNaturalist provides coordinates, slight inaccuracies or intentional blurring (e.g., for sensitive species) can occur. Our analysis relies on the provided coordinates, and these maps represent the reported locations.
  • **Buffer Zone Calculation:** Buffer zones were calculated using standard geospatial libraries (e.g., Shapely, GeoPandas) around the precise coordinates of the SludgeHub Property Center and the Apiary Center. These are approximations and reflect straight-line distances.
  • **Map Interactivity:** Maps are generated using Folium, providing interactive zooming and panning. Clicking on individual observation markers may reveal more details, depending on the data available from iNaturalist.
